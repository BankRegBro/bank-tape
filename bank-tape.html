How these numbers are made

Figures are bank-level from the FFIEC call report of the stated period; the traded entity is the holding company, so for institutions with large nonbank operations the ratios run rich. Tangible book is total equity less goodwill and other intangibles. Adjusted tangible book further deducts the pre-tax unrealized loss on held-to-maturity securities (amortized cost less fair value, Schedule RC-B), the mark the balance sheet is allowed to omit.

Deposit premium is (market cap − TBV) / core deposits, the classic acquirer's yardstick. The warranted multiple is (ROTE − g) / (COE − g) with the assumptions shown above the table; "vs warranted" is the actual multiple minus that figure, the market's verdict in one number. "Vs peers" is the residual from a cross-sectional fit of the multiple on ROTE, uninsured deposit share, and size across this universe: cheap or rich given the profile, refit on every load. The detail rows carry a modeled between-quarters book value built from the earnings run-rate net of dividends only; it is labeled as a model and excludes rate drift. The downstream column is bank-level tangible equity divided by the holding company's consolidated tangible common equity. Above 100% the subsidiary carries more tangible equity than the entire group's common, which is only possible where the parent has funded it with capital that is not common equity, ordinarily preferred stock or holding company debt: double leverage, measured rather than inferred. Below 100% the group holds tangible common outside the lead charter. The reading is checkable against banks whose goodwill runs a third to a half of equity and which still land within ten points of parity, since that cannot occur unless bank-level goodwill is being deducted correctly. Where SEC filings supply it, the detail rows carry a holdco check: tangible common equity from the 10-Q or 10-K, the multiple on that consolidated book, and how much of it the lead bank's call report actually covers. Multiples marked "h" are priced on the holdco book. The book-gap axis is scaled to the multiples actually present in the universe, so a bank carrying a large held-to-maturity mark is never clipped at the frame. Banks marked multi-charter belong to a holding company that runs several bank charters; their book-value columns are withheld rather than estimated, since one charter's call report cannot be compared to the whole company's market value. Prices are prior-close; call report data ages between quarters and each row says exactly how much.
